- Q: How frequently should live game scores update during active games? → A: Every 60 seconds, automatically without page refresh. - Ideally as fast as possible, allowing for people to watch games and if they want information regarding something they just saw the site would be updated within seconds of the event occurring.  If an action happens within the game (e.g. a goal that could still go under review) mark the play as pending until it is 100% confirmed.


- Q: What should the hamburger menu contain in this phase beyond the dark mode toggle? → A: Dark mode toggle and a league selector (preparing for future multi-league expansion). - The hamburger menu will show Account, Settings and League Selector.  It is possible some sections, like games, will be moved from the banner to the hamburger bar.  There may also be added sections like Donations, Feedback, Site Mission, etc.


- Q: How frequently should non-live data (standings, stats, rosters, trades, free agents) refresh from the data source? → A: Standings and rosters refresh hourly; stats, trades, and free agents refresh every 3 hours. - Standings, stats and rosters should update just before and after each game.  Trades and free agents should update as soon as possible, allowing for people to view it like a broker would watch a stock ticker.  When a signing or trade is confirmed but all of the information is not known, put any available information with a note to the user that more information is still processing. Use this directive for all future statements regarding frequency of data.


- Q: How far back should historical data extend for player career stats, trade history, and team records? → A: Since the 2000-01 season (25 years of data, covering most active player careers and the modern analytics era).  - As far back as reliable data can go. Differentiate eras in some way (background shading or bold separation lines etc.) The eras are between the original 6 to 1972 (when 15 or fewer teams were in the league), 1973 to 2005, and 2006 to present (salary cap era).


- Q: How should video content be delivered when a visitor clicks to watch a play? → A: Embed video from the data provider (e.g., NHL.com or Sportradar embed URLs) in a modal player on-site. - Include videos with multiple replays from multiple angles.

#### Global Design & Navigation


- **FR-005a**: The global search bar MUST search across all entity types (players, teams, trades, staff, etc.) and display results grouped by entity type with clickable links to the relevant pages. - As results appear, favor the most searched categories first followed by alphabetical order.  The search bar is only meant to function for the selected league so as not to clutter the results across all leagues when a user is navigating a specific league.  There is to be no search bar on the home screen with the lists of leagues.


- **FR-011d**: Standings and roster data MUST refresh from the data source at least once per hour. Stats, trades, and free agent data MUST refresh at least once every 3 hours. Live game scores continue to refresh every 60 seconds per FR-016/SC-016. - All listed entities are to be refreshed as fast as possible for competitive information seekers.


- **FR-016**: Game score boxes MUST display in a 4-column grid, ordered by start time with ties broken by alphabetical order of the home team's city. - Include an extremely abbreviated score box between the top black bar and the tabs (scores, standings, stats etc.) that only shows the icons of the team and the score with the period and game clock. This is to appear on any page the user navigates to within that league with the game clock counting down with the actual game event.


#### Game Hub


- **FR-025**: Goal summaries MUST appear to the left (away team) and right (home team) of the central stats, showing period/time, scorer (first initial and last name) with season goal number, and assists with season assist numbers. - Include the name of the player(s) with assists (first initial and last name) in the same manner.


- **FR-026**: Penalty summaries MUST appear beneath each team's goal summaries, showing period/time, offending player, penalty type, and the player's season penalty minute total. - The offending player is to appear as first initial and last name.


- **FR-031**: The Player Stats tab MUST display two side-by-side grids (away left, home right) with skater stats: #, G, A, PTS, +/-, HIT, PIM, TOI, SOG, FO%, GV, TK, WAR, xGF/60. - Make FO% last.


- **FR-034**: The Rink Instances tab MUST display an accurately dimensioned diagram of the game's rink, including measurements and labels for the home bench and away bench sides. - Include penalty boxes and an accurate view of the layout of the stands and the actual colors of the seats if information is available. If the information is unavailable, make an outline of the stands according to the best information available and make the seats appear as a washed out gray if the coloring is unavailable.


- **FR-037**: Hovering over a rink dot MUST display the action type, player, and game clock time; clicking a dot with available video MUST open a modal containing an embedded video player (sourced from the data provider) with footage of the play. - Include an unintrusive, clean indicator within the dots that have related video footage.  Do not make it the traditional triangle play button, make it even less intrusive and rely on the user to learn that the simple indicator displayed means there is a video associated with it.  Use a small black dot within the colored dot with the capability to change this if needed.
