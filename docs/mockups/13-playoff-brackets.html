Matchup Summary

Carolina Hurricanes — Strengths & Weaknesses

Carolina's relentless forechecking and top-five penalty kill defined their regular season. Their defensive structure under Rod Brind'Amour limits quality chances, and Svechnikov's 38-goal campaign gives them a legitimate first-line finisher. Their weakness lies in inconsistent goaltending in high-event games and a power play that went cold down the stretch.

New York Rangers — Strengths & Weaknesses

The Rangers lean heavily on Igor Shesterkin's ability to steal games. Their top six is skilled enough to compete with anyone when engaged, and their transition game off the rush is dangerous. However, their bottom six provides little secondary scoring, and their defensive zone coverage has been exploitable all season, particularly on the forecheck.

How the Hurricanes Win

Carolina's forechecking style is built for the playoffs. If they can sustain pressure, force turnovers, and keep the Rangers' top line cycling in the defensive zone, their depth advantage takes over. Winning the special teams battle — keeping their PK sharp while exploiting New York's shaky penalty kill — would be decisive.

How the Rangers Create an Upset

Shesterkin would need to be transcendent, and the Rangers would need to play a disciplined, low-event game that limits Carolina's transition opportunities. If Panarin and Zibanejad find their playoff gear and the Rangers can convert on the power play, they have the talent to make this a coin flip. Anything can happen in playoff hockey — the regular season matchups don't always carry forward when intensity escalates.

Matchup Summary

Carolina Hurricanes — Strengths & Weaknesses

Carolina's first-round dominance wasn't surprising — their forechecking system dismantled the Rangers' breakout completely. Svechnikov continued his hot streak (5G, 3A in 6 games) and their penalty kill was a fortress at 84.6%. The concern is fatigue management; they ran heavy minutes through their top four defensemen.

Toronto Maple Leafs — Strengths & Weaknesses

Toronto finally broke through in a Game 7, and the confidence from overcoming Washington could be transformative. Matthews was dominant (6G, 4A in 7 games) and their power play clicked at 22.7%. The seven-game grind could leave them tired, but it also battle-tested their depth in ways the regular season couldn't.

How the Hurricanes Win

Same formula, amplified: suffocate Toronto's transition game, force turnovers in the neutral zone, and make Matthews work for every inch of ice. If Carolina's goaltending stays above .920 and they continue winning the special teams battle, their systemic advantage should prevail over a longer series.

How the Maple Leafs Create an Upset

Matthews vs. Carolina's top pair is the series. If he can generate enough five-on-five offense to ease the burden on the power play, Toronto's skill advantage at the top of the lineup could shine through. Nylander needs to be a factor — he was inconsistent against Washington. And this is playoff hockey: seven-game survivors carry a different kind of belief into the next round.
