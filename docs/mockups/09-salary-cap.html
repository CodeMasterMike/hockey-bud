Cap Explained

CBA language side by side with plain-language explanations. Search for specific terms below.

Upper Limit (Salary Cap)
CBA / Legal Text
"The Upper Limit for each League Year shall be the Midpoint plus the Range. No Club at any point during the League Year may have an Averaged Club Salary that exceeds the Upper Limit. The determination of Upper Limit is based on Hockey Related Revenue as defined in Article 50." — CBA Article 50.5(b)
Plain Language
The salary cap is the maximum amount a team can spend on player salaries in a given season. It is calculated from total league revenue. For 2025-26, it is set at $88,000,000. No team may exceed this amount at any point during the season, though mechanisms like LTIR (Long-Term Injured Reserve) provide some relief for injured player salaries.
LTIR (Long-Term Injured Reserve)
CBA / Legal Text
"If a Player has been on Injured Reserve for not less than twenty-four (24) days and has missed not less than ten (10) consecutive Regular Season Games, the Club may designate the Player as a Long-Term Injured Reserve Player. Upon such designation, the Club's Averaged Club Salary may exceed the Upper Limit by the Player's Averaged Amount." — CBA Article 50.10(d)
Plain Language
When a player is injured long-term (out at least 24 days and 10 games), the team can place them on LTIR. This effectively allows the team to exceed the salary cap by the injured player's cap hit, giving them room to call up replacement players. However, LTIR does not truly "remove" the cap hit — it provides conditional relief that can limit a team's ability to bank cap space.
Performance Bonuses
CBA / Legal Text
"Performance Bonuses may be included in Entry-Level SPCs and in SPCs of Players who are 35 years of age or older, and in SPCs of Players who have returned from a career-threatening injury or illness. Individual Performance Bonus criteria must be reasonable and achievable. The aggregate amount of all Performance Bonuses in a single season shall not exceed the lesser of 7.5% of Paragraph 1 compensation or $850,000." — CBA Article 50.2(b)(iii)
Plain Language
Young players on entry-level contracts (and some veterans) can earn extra money through performance bonuses. If a team exceeds the salary cap because a player hits their bonuses, the overage is applied to the following season's cap. For example, if a rookie earns $500,000 in bonuses that push the team $200,000 over the cap, that $200,000 penalty carries over to the next year.
Retained Salary in Trades
CBA / Legal Text
"In connection with the Trade of a Player, the Traded From Club may elect to retain a portion of the Player's Averaged Amount. Such retained Salary Benefit may not exceed 50% of the Player's Averaged Amount at the time of the trade. A Club may not have more than three (3) retained Salary Benefit agreements outstanding at any one time." — CBA Article 50.5(d)(ii)
Plain Language
When trading a player, a team can agree to continue paying up to 50% of the player's cap hit. This counts against the trading team's cap, not the receiving team's. A team can only retain salary on a maximum of three contracts at a time. This mechanism is often used to facilitate trades involving high-salaried players.
Salary Cap Glossary
AAV (Average Annual Value)
The total contract value divided by its length. This is the figure that counts against the salary cap each year, regardless of actual salary paid in any given season.
Cap Hit
The amount a player's contract counts against the team's salary cap. Equals the AAV of the contract plus any applicable performance bonus overages from the prior season.
Dead Cap
Cap charge for a player no longer on the roster, typically from a buyout or retained salary in a trade. The team carries this hit even though the player is gone.
LTIR (Long-Term Injured Reserve)
A designation for players injured 24+ days and 10+ games. Provides conditional cap relief equal to the player's cap hit, allowing roster flexibility.
NMC (No Movement Clause)
Contract clause preventing a team from trading, waiving, or sending a player to the minors without the player's consent. The most restrictive form of trade protection.
NTC (No Trade Clause)
Contract clause requiring the player's approval before being traded to another team. Does not protect against waivers or minor-league assignment.
UFA (Unrestricted Free Agent)
A player who is free to sign with any team once their contract expires. Generally applies to players age 27 or older, or with 7+ years of NHL service.
RFA (Restricted Free Agent)
A player whose contract has expired but whose current team retains the right to match any offer sheet from another team. Younger players with limited service time.
